Dr. John Smith | posted 4 hours ago

Hi Mumthumb

Sorry to hear that you've had a bad experience with a doctor who really doesn't understand how to look after a patient holistically. Sadly there are many great doctors who obviously omitted the compassionate gene in their makeup and perhaps didn't attend any training on it either! But often they're very good surgeons or specialists so it can be hard to know when/how to complain!

I too was diagnosed with a rare sarcoma, cystosarcoma phyllodes and understand that often there is little information to hand or leaflets to refer to or indeed finding others with the same diagnosis. The apparent lack of understanding and support sometimes feels like too much to bear on your own. However what I've discovered is the joys of the internet, finding people via their blog entries and online support groups and forums such as this one. I also attend a support group for sarcoma patients in London (in association with Sarcoma UK). The next meeting is on Thursday evening. I'm not sure where you're based but do, if you can, pop along to the meeting. There are some amazing people who attend, all of whom have been diagnosed or supporting someone with a Sarcoma and lots or rare versions at that, so I'm sure they'll fully empathise and may be able to help. Sadly I can't be there this month. I will privately message you the contact details and directions etc. If you're not in London however there are other support groups that you may find helpful to attend.

Dr. Smith


Patient response | posted 2 hours ago

Hi mumthumb

If only these doctors realise the damage they do when they are unable to treat vulnerable patients with consideration! There we are, scared to death, probably not used to hospitals, wondering what will happen next and along comes a member of the medical staff who hasn't any people skills at all (they behave like morons) and because we are so emotional, it has a very deep effect on us. No, you're not alone, as many on this site will testify but try to see it as his shortcoming not yours. I've been in the "system" for so long, I find it easy to be very appreciative to staff who treat me well but soon tell others if they are less than caring. I have to say, I have been very lucky with hospital staff and my own GP practice but I know this isn't the case for everyone.

Try not to let this get you down because of a person who obviously needs brushing up on his bedside manner (or perhaps he's in the wrong job!) and I'll glad to hear you've found your fighting spirit again. I hope someone with your type of cancer comes along for you to exchange experiences but in the meantime, keep coming back as we're all in this together.

Love

Carrie
